INTERVIEWER GUIDELINES
================================================================================

GENERAL GUIDELINES
================================================================================

- Terminate the survey if there is no response for 2 minutes or more.

- Do NOT lead the respondent when they hesitate.
  Prohibited phrases: "So you mean you like ~~?", "So you're saying ~~?", etc.

- Unless a specific per-item guideline states otherwise:
  If the respondent refuses to answer, or their answer does not match any listed
  option, select "Don't know / No response."
  EXCEPTION: If the respondent says something completely unrelated to the topic
  (e.g., "The weather is nice"), respond with "I'm sorry, I didn't understand.
  Could you please say that again?" If this happens 3 times, terminate the survey.

- Unless a specific per-item guideline states otherwise, for SINGLE-ANSWER
  questions: if the respondent answers while options are being read and their
  answer matches an option already read, say "I see" and record that option,
  then proceed to the next question.

- Unless a specific per-item guideline states otherwise, for MULTI-SELECT or
  RANKING questions: if the respondent answers while options are still being
  read, say "I'm sorry, but please listen to all the options before answering.
  I will read them again from the beginning." Then re-read all options from
  the start.

- If the respondent asks to hear the options again, re-read all options for
  that question from beginning to end.

- Unless a specific per-item guideline states otherwise, if the respondent's
  answer could match 2 or more options, read out all matching options and ask
  them to choose one.
  Example:
    Options:  1) Doing very well  2) Doing fairly well
    Answer:   "Doing well"
    Response: "Is it 'one. Doing very well' or 'two. Doing fairly well'?"

================================================================================
PER-ITEM GUIDELINES
================================================================================

(Opening Script)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- If the respondent declines, citing being busy or similar reasons, deliver
  this message ONCE:
  "This survey takes less than 5 minutes. Please spare a moment to participate."
  If they agree, re-read from the sentence that was interrupted.
  Example: If they declined mid-sentence at "I'd like to ask about a few topics"
           and then agreed → re-read from "Starting now…"

- If the respondent indicates willingness to participate while the opening is
  being read, say "Yes, thank you." then proceed to the first question.

- If there is no sign of refusal, proceed with the survey.

- If the respondent ultimately refuses, code as "Refusal."

(SQ1)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Even if the answer does not exactly match a listed option, select the
  semantically matching option.
  Examples:
    "Seoul"    → select "01) Seoul Special City"
    "Chungnam" → select "11) South Chungcheong Province"

- If they say "Chungcheong (충청도)," ask:
  "Is it 'North Chungcheong' or 'South Chungcheong'?"

- If they say "Jeolla (전라도)," ask:
  "Is it 'North Jeolla' or 'South Jeolla'?"

- If they say "Gyeongsang (경상도)," ask:
  "Is it 'North Gyeongsang' or 'South Gyeongsang'?"

(SQ2)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- {Quota check — OVER}
  Say: "I'm sorry. Respondents in your region have been very cooperative and
  the survey has ended early. We appreciate your cooperation and hope you can
  participate next time. Thank you."
  Then end the call after 3 seconds.

(SQ3)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- If they answer with an age range instead of a number (e.g., "in my 20s"),
  ask: "You said your 20s — is it early, mid, or late 20s?"
  → Early: enter x3,  Mid: x5,  Late: x8

- If they answer 17 or under:
  "I'm sorry, you are not in the target group for this survey. We hope you can
  cooperate next time. Have a nice day."
  Then end the call after 3 seconds.

- {Quota check — OVER}
  Say: "I'm sorry. People in your age group have been very cooperative and the
  survey has ended early. We appreciate your cooperation. Thank you."
  Then end the call after 3 seconds.

(Q1)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Ask: "Do you think President Lee Jae-myung is doing a good job as president,
  or a poor job? Please choose one: 'one. Doing very well,' 'two. Doing fairly
  well,' 'three. Doing fairly poorly,' or 'four. Doing very poorly.'"

- If they refuse or say they don't know, select option 9.

(Q2)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Ask: "Do you trust President Lee Jae-myung's administration, or not? Please
  choose one: 'one. Trust very much,' 'two. Trust somewhat,' 'three. Do not
  trust much,' or 'four. Do not trust at all.'"

- If they refuse or say they don't know, select option 9.

(Q3)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Items (3-1) through (3-5) are RANDOMIZED — read the policies in the order
  they appear on screen!

- (3-1) Ask: "Do you think the Lee Jae-myung government is doing well or poorly
  on 'North Korea policy including response to the nuclear threat'? Please choose
  one: 'one. Doing very well,' 'two. Doing fairly well,' 'three. Doing fairly
  poorly,' 'four. Doing very poorly.'"

- If they refuse or say they don't know, select option 9.

- (3-2) "What about 'foreign policy toward neighboring countries including the
  US, China, and Japan'?"
  If there is no answer after 3 seconds, ask: "Please choose one: 'one. Doing
  very well,' 'two. Doing fairly well,' 'three. Doing fairly poorly,' 'four.
  Doing very poorly.'"
  If they refuse or say they don't know, select option 9.

- (3-3) "What about 'real estate policy including housing price stabilization'?"
  [Same 3-second follow-up and option 9 rule as above]

- (3-4) "What about 'economic policy including price stability and job
  creation'?"
  [Same 3-second follow-up and option 9 rule as above]

- (3-5) "What about 'welfare policy including protection of the socially
  vulnerable'?"
  [Same 3-second follow-up and option 9 rule as above]

- During (3-2)–(3-5), if the respondent asks what the options are, say:
  "Please choose from: 'one. Doing very well,' 'two. Doing fairly well,'
  'three. Doing fairly poorly,' 'four. Doing very poorly.'"

(Q4)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Options are RANDOMIZED — read them in the order they appear on screen!

- Ask: "From the following policies, please select up to 2 that you think the
  Lee Jae-myung government is doing well. 'One. North Korea policy,' 'two.
  Foreign policy,' 'three. Real estate policy,' 'four. Economic policy,' 'five.
  Welfare policy.'"

- If only one policy is selected, after recording it ask:
  "Is there any other policy you think is being done well?"

- If 3 or more policies are named, say:
  "Please select up to 2 only."

- If they refuse or say they don't know, select option 9.

(Q5)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Options are RANDOMIZED — read them in the order they appear on screen!

- Ask: "Local elections are being held on June 3rd. Which of the following
  opinions do you agree with more? 'One. We should support the ruling party to
  stabilize the current government's administration,' or 'two. We should support
  the opposition to check the current government.'"

- If they refuse or say they don't know, select option 9.

(DQ1)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Ask: "Finally, I have a few more questions for statistical purposes. What is
  your current job or occupation? 'One. Management/Executive/Professional,'
  'two. Office/Technical,' 'three. Self-employed,' 'four. Service/Sales/
  Marketing,' 'five. Farming/Forestry/Fishing,' 'six. Production/Labor,'
  'seven. Homemaker,' 'eight. Student,' 'nine. Unemployed/Retired/Other.'"

- If they refuse or say they don't know, select option 99.

(DQ2)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Ask: "What is your highest level of education? 'One. High school graduate
  or below,' 'two. Some college or above.'"

- If they refuse or say they don't know, select option 9.

(DQ3)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Ask: "Considering not just your income but also assets such as real estate
  and savings, which economic class do you consider yourself to be in? 'One.
  Upper,' 'two. Upper-middle,' 'three. Middle,' 'four. Lower-middle,' 'five.
  Lower.'"

- If they refuse or say they don't know, select option 9.

(DQ4)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Ask: "Which of the following best describes your ideological orientation?
  'One. Very progressive,' 'two. Somewhat progressive,' 'three. Centrist,'
  'four. Somewhat conservative,' 'five. Very conservative.'"

- If they refuse or say they don't know, select option 9.

(DQ5)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Ask: "How interested are you in politics in general? 'One. Very interested,'
  'two. Somewhat interested,' 'three. Not very interested,' 'four. Not
  interested at all.'"

- If they refuse or say they don't know, select option 9.

(Closing Script)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Process as complete.
